The Hidden Cost of Poor CE Tracking

A lapsed massage therapy license means you stop working — immediately. No appointments, no income, no exceptions. Yet thousands of LMTs scramble every renewal cycle because they didn't track their continuing education hours.

The average massage therapist spends $500-1,200 per year on continuing education and holds licenses in 1.7 states on average (many hold 2-3, especially near state borders). Each state has different hour requirements, different approved providers, and different renewal cycles. Missing a single requirement means your license goes inactive.

Common CE Compliance Mistakes

  1. Losing certificates. A provider emailed you a PDF certificate 18 months ago. Is it still in your inbox? Your Downloads folder?
  2. Forgetting special requirements. Ethics hours, HIV/AIDS training, law updates — these specific requirements are easy to overlook in a stack of 24+ CE hours.
  3. Multi-state confusion. If you hold licenses in 2+ states, you need to track separate requirements for each. A CE course approved in one state may not count in another.
  4. Last-minute panic. Searching for approved CE courses 2 weeks before renewal means paying rush fees and taking courses that aren't your first choice.

Build the Habit

Five minutes per CE course is all it takes:

  1. When you finish a course, log it immediately (provider, date, hours, category)
  2. Upload the certificate or take a photo
  3. Tag which states it applies to
  4. Check your progress dashboard quarterly
  5. Set a 90-day pre-renewal alert
