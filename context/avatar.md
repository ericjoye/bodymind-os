# Avatar — Independent Massage Therapist

## Who they are
- Licensed Massage Therapist (LMT) in their state
- 28-55 years old, ~70% female
- Completed 500-1000 hours of training at an approved program
- Operates solo: mobile (travels to clients), home-based, or rents a single room
- Sees 15-30 clients/week at $80-150/session
- Annual revenue: $40,000-$75,000

## Job-to-be-done
Run a professional massage practice without the overhead of a clinic manager. Get booked, stay organized, protect my license, and reduce no-shows.

## What they feel
- 🟡 PROVISIONAL: Frustrated by cobbling together Instagram + Google Calendar + paper forms
- 🟡 PROVISIONAL: Anxious about missing CE deadlines and losing their license
- 🟡 PROVISIONAL: Worried about liability — what if a client has a condition they didn't disclose?
- 🟢 REAL: Professional pride — they're a healthcare provider, not just a "service worker"
- 🟡 PROVISIONAL: Overwhelmed by enterprise software that's built for spas with 10+ staff

## What they already use
- Instagram/Facebook for marketing and client communication
- Google Calendar or pen-and-paper for scheduling
- Paper intake forms (clipboard + pen in the waiting area)
- Square, Venmo, or cash for payments
- Notebook for session notes
- Spreadsheet or nothing for CE tracking

## What would make them NOT buy
- If it's too complex ("I just need to book clients, not run a CRM")
- If it takes commission on their bookings (they already pay Square 2.9%)
- If it doesn't work on their phone (they're on their feet all day)
- If it looks/feels like enterprise software (they chose solo practice to escape that)
- If it's more than $30/month (that's 2+ sessions gone to software)

## What would make them buy
- A booking page that clients can use 24/7 (no more texting back and forth)
- Digital intake forms that flag health risks automatically
- CE tracking that tells them exactly how many hours they need before renewal
- Session notes that are searchable and professional
- Something that makes them look more professional than the therapist down the street

## Key psychology
- They're healthcare providers first, business owners second
- License = livelihood. Anything that protects the license is worth paying for
- They chose solo practice for independence — software must feel like freedom, not bureaucracy
- Word-of-mouth in massage therapy schools and professional associations is powerful
